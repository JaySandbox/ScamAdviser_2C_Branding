Do

  • Lead with the verb. "check that link" not "it is recommended that you check."
  • Lowercase casual headers when the context is chill.
  • Use concrete numbers. "12/100" beats "low."
  • Be self-aware about AI. Name it as Sharp, not a buzzword.
  • Quote the community. "2,341 people reported this" > "our data shows."

Don't

  • Fear-monger. "You'll lose everything" is cringe and Gen Z spots fake urgency instantly.
  • Reach for jargon. "Threat vector" belongs in a B2B deck, not an app.
  • Sound institutional. "We advise you to…" — no one talks like that.
  • Explain the tech unprompted. Nobody asked about the algorithm.
  • Moralize. We're not the online-safety police.

Photography direction

Real people, ordinary moments — caught mid-scroll, mid-laugh, mid-eye-roll at a sketchy DM.

Yes — shoot this

  • Candid phone scrolling on a couch, in bed, at a café
  • Natural light. Morning windows. Golden hour if earned.
  • Friends showing each other a screen ("wait, is this legit?")
  • Hands, half-faces, over-the-shoulder angles
  • Messy everyday objects — unfinished coffee, tangled earbuds
  • A real age range. Not just 22-year-olds with perfect teeth.

No — never shoot this

  • Hoodie hacker in a dark room with green code reflection
  • Glowing padlock icons floating over blue circuit backgrounds
  • Suit-and-briefcase "security professional" stock imagery
  • Red alarm overlays or siren graphics on screens
  • Binary "1010101" as a design motif. It's 2026.
  • Fear-face reactions. The vibe is aware, not panicked.
